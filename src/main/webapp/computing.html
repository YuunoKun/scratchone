    Why computing?

    Computing is a faculty that teaches programming skill and its application. Major computing disciplines include computer science,computer engineering, information security, information systems, and business analytics.

    Students will be trained to be a problem solver. They will get to explore and develop cutting edge technology and apply them in almost all industries in today's technological world.

    A computing student should be:

    1. Hard-working
    2. Problem-solver
    3. Logical
    4. Passionate
    5. Curious
    6. Self-learner

    Frequently Asked Question

    What are the differences among computer science, information system, business analytics, information security and computing engineering?

    They are all majors under computing faculty. Computer science is more theoretically and it covers a wider range of technical skills compared to information system and business analytics.

    Information system is all about designing, implementing and managing IT. It focuses on incorporating IT skills into business industries.

    Business Analytics teaches statistical and analytical methods that make up the backbone of data science. Student will work with spreadsheets, aggregate data, evaluate statistical significance, and determine statistical trends.

    Information Security is all about keeping computer systems safe. As a student of this programme, you'll learn to identify vulnerabilities in systems, develop security protocols, and respond to attempted breaches.

    Computer Engineering lies at the intersection between computing and electronics. While Computer Science students dedicate most of their time to programming and software, Computer Engineering students spend most of their time working on electronics and hardware.

    What should I do to better prepare myself for a future computing major?

    Computing is a very competitive major. You need to be confident with your Maths and train yourself to be a logical thinker.

    You also need to be patient, meticulous and be able to handle stress (especially when you have to debug).

    What are my future job prospect?

    Computing graduate are highly demanded in all industries. You can enjoy a fairly good pay with a computing degree.

    Your possible career path could be Software Engineer, Data Scientist, Game Developer, Finance Analyst, Market Research Analyst and more
