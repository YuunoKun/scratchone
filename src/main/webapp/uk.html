    Why UK?

    The UK is one of the world’s most popular destinations to study higher education, with more than 500,000 international students enrolling each year. UK universities are among the best in the world, and consistently perform well in world rankings. They also have a reputation for world-class research. UK courses are generally shorter than other countries (three years for undegraduate, one or two year for master).It can be possible to work while you study too.

    UK higher education applications are made through the Universities and Colleges Admissions Service (UCAS).

    Key dates for application
    19 May

    Start of application

    UCAS Undergraduate Apply opens for 2021 entry.

    15 Oct

    Deadline for certain schools and courses

    Applications for Oxford, Cambridge and most courses in medicine, dentistry, and veterinary medicine/science should arrive at UCAS by 18:00 (UK time).

    15 Jan

    Deadline for most schools and courses

    Applications for the majority of undergraduate courses should arrive at UCAS by 18:00 (UK time) on this day (check course details in our search tool for the correct deadline).

    31 Aug

    Deadline for conditions

    The deadline for any remaining conditions to be met – otherwise the university or college might not accept the applicant. Adjustment ends on this date.


    Frequently Asked Question

    How much does it cost to study in UK (sgd)

    Tuition fees for international students vary depending on the course you’re studying. It can range from $23,000 to $99,000 for different courses and schools. Most courses should cost around $40,000.

    The basic cost of living in London can range from $1900 to $2300 per month.

    The basic cost of living outside of London can range from $1400 to $1800 per month.

    The total annual cost can range from $35,600 to $115,200. For most courses, the annual cost should range from $52,600 to $60,700

    What should I prepare for UCAS application.

    To apply, you will need to prepare predicted grades forecasted by your teachers and referee letters written by your referee.

    If you are applying for Oxford and Cambridge, you need to register for the examination and interview.

    You may have to go through interview and examination if you are applying for courses such as Law and Medicine from other Universities.

    When does the semester starts?

    It usually starts around September for semester 1 and January for semester 2.

    Employment

    Jobs for International students are plenty after graduating. From September 2020 any student enrolling at a UK university can stay in the UK after graduation for up to two years in order to look for work related to their degree or course.

    Once they have found a job, they will need to apply for Tier 2 visa which will allow them to stay for a maximum of 5 years and 14 days, or the time given on your certificate of sponsorship plus 1 month, whichever is shorter.
